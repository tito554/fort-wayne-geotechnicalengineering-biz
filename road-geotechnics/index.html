Road geotechnics in Fort Wayne addresses the interaction between pavement systems and the region’s glacial till, lacustrine clays, and alluvial soils. Local practice follows AASHTO and INDOT specifications, requiring thorough subgrade evaluation before any pavement commitment. A reliable CBR study for road design quantifies bearing capacity and moisture susceptibility, directly informing structural layer thickness. For asphalt-surfaced corridors, flexible pavement design adapts to these variable ground conditions while meeting Indiana’s traffic load criteria.

This expertise supports arterial reconstructions, industrial access roads, and residential subdivisions where weak soils demand engineered stabilization. Municipal projects and INDOT-maintained routes often integrate rigid pavement design for high-volume intersections and bus lanes, ensuring long-term durability over Fort Wayne’s frost-active subgrade. Comprehensive geotechnical input reduces differential settlement and extends pavement service life across the city’s diverse soil profiles.
